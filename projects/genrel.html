GenRel

GenRel or Genetic Relation Detection is a machine learning project focused on identifying the existence of genetic relationships between the subjects of a photograph. The project leverages machine learning models to analyze images and detect familial connections among individuals present in the photos.

Key Features:

  1. Machine Learning Models: The project employs machine learning models, including Multilayer Perceptron Classifier and Random Forest Classifier, to analyze image data and classify genetic relationships.
  2. Dataset: Genetic Relation Detection utilizes the Kinship Faces in the Wild dataset, which contains a wide range of images featuring family members. This dataset serves as the foundation for training and testing the models.
  3. Accuracy Improvement: The project includes efforts to fine-tune hyperparameters and optimize the machine learning models, resulting in improved accuracy in detecting genetic relationships.

Tech Stack:

  • Python
  • Google Colab

    • Fun Fact:

    This project was developed as part of the Introduction to Artificial Intelligence course, during which an ex-FBI agent was invited to talk to us about the importance of genetic identification in catching suspects. He gave us the example of the Golden State Killer who was caught after 40 years after DNA analysis of a tissue found in his trash!
